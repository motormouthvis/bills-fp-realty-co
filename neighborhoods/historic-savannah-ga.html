Historic Savannah

A living urban neighborhood inside the nation's largest historic district

Twenty-Two Squares of American History

Founded in 1733, Savannah's Historic District is the largest National Historic Landmark District in the country. Twenty-two original Oglethorpe squares form the heart of the district, each shaded by 250-year-old live oaks and surrounded by Federal-era and Victorian rowhomes, walled gardens, and converted carriage houses.

While the district draws millions of visitors annually, it is also a real working neighborhood. Locals walk to the riverfront for coffee, raise families in restored townhomes, and gather for Friday-night concerts in Forsyth Park. Architecture-loving buyers gravitate here for the chance to own a piece of 18th- and 19th-century America.

Why Buyers Choose the District
  • Walking access to 22 historic squares and Forsyth Park
  • Carefully preserved Federal, Greek Revival, and Italianate architecture
  • Vibrant restaurant, gallery, and music scene
  • Riverfront and SCAD campus a few blocks away
Plan Your Visit

Walking tours depart from our district office every Saturday at 10am.
