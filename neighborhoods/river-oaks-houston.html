River Oaks

Houston's historic luxury neighborhood

A Century of Houston Luxury

River Oaks was master-planned in 1924 by the Hogg brothers as Houston's premier residential district, with a Will Hare street layout, deed-restricted lot sizes, and a country club at its heart. A hundred years later, it remains the city's most coveted address — home to a who's-who of Houston business and society and to some of the largest historic estates in Texas.

The neighborhood is anchored by the River Oaks Country Club, the River Oaks Shopping Center (the oldest planned shopping center in the city), and the River Oaks Theatre. The streets feature an unusual mix of Georgian, Tudor, Italianate, and modern estates — most updated within the last two decades but retaining their original footprints.

Grand homes on oversized lots, an active country club, and walking access to the River Oaks Shopping Center and the historic theater.

Zoned to River Oaks Elementary, Lanier Middle, and Lamar High School. Top private options include St. John's, Kinkaid, and The Village School.
