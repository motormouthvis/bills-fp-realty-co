Uptown Charlotte

Charlotte's high-rise core, where commerce, culture, and sports converge

A Vertical Neighborhood

Uptown is what Charlotte calls its downtown — a 1.6-square-mile core that anchors the second-largest banking center in the United States and an increasingly diverse residential community. The neighborhood is divided into four wards, each with its own character, and laid out around a central public square at Trade and Tryon Streets.

Residents have walking access to the Bank of America Stadium, the Spectrum Center arena, the Mint Museum, the Levine Museum of the New South, Discovery Place science museum, and the Romare Bearden Park. The CATS LYNX Blue Line light rail runs the spine of the district, connecting to South End and the University area.

A useful Uptown reference point: many maps and ride-share trips center on the historic Bank of America Corporate Center near 100 N Tryon St, Charlotte, NC. That's a city landmark and not the location of our neighborhood sales office, which is shown to the right.
