West Loop

Chicago's hottest urban submarket

From Meat-Packing to Michelin Stars

Once Chicago's wholesale food district, the West Loop has reinvented itself as the city's most-talked-about neighborhood — home to Restaurant Row, the Fulton Market food hall scene, McDonald's global HQ, Google's Chicago office, and a dozen Michelin-recommended restaurants packed into eight blocks.

Housing ranges from sleek new-construction condo towers to converted timber-loft buildings with original cast-iron facades. Empty-nesters relocating from the suburbs and young professionals dominate the buyer pool, drawn by the walk-everywhere lifestyle and direct CTA access to the Loop.

Highlights
  • Restaurant Row along Randolph Street
  • Fulton Market food halls and rooftop bars
  • Direct CTA Pink and Green Line access
  • Skinner West and Galileo schools in district
