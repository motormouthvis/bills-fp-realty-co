Kentlands

A celebrated New Urbanism community in Maryland

A Walkable American Town, By Design

Kentlands is one of the most influential New Urbanism communities ever built — a 352-acre experiment in returning to the small-town American pattern of front porches, rear alleys, mixed-use Main Streets, and walkable schools. Designed in 1988 by Duany Plater-Zyberk on the former Kent family farm, the community remains a benchmark studied by planners around the world.

Homes line tree-shaded streets in a tight grid, with detached garages tucked behind on alleys. The Kentlands Mansion serves as the community house and event space. Just to the north, the Kentlands Market Square hosts a grocery, a movie theater, restaurants, a co-working space, and a farmers market each Thursday from May to October.
