Highland Park

The Park Cities' most prestigious address

A Storied Texas Enclave

Highland Park is a small, independent town entirely surrounded by Dallas, founded in 1913 by the Armstrong family on land that had been a cotton plantation. The original Wilbur David Cook street plan — the same designer behind Beverly Hills — remains essentially intact, with serpentine streets, generous setbacks, and a thirty-mile network of pedestrian-only paths along Turtle Creek.

The town runs its own award-winning school district, its own police and fire departments, and operates Highland Park Village — widely considered the nation's first planned shopping center and still home to flagship boutiques from Hermes, Chanel, and Christian Louboutin. Real estate ranges from restored 1920s estates on Beverly Drive to discreet new-construction homes priced into eight figures.
