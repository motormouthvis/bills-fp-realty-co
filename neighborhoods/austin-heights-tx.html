The Austin Heights Neighborhood

A Local's Guide

Austin Heights is one of those neighborhoods that practically defines the city. You'll find tin-roof bungalows sitting shoulder to shoulder with modernist new builds, music venues that have been around since the seventies, and food trucks parked under live oaks on every block.

Most weekends start with breakfast tacos and end on a patio along South Lamar. The neighborhood centers around the strip near 2400 S Lamar Blvd, Austin, TX 78704, where a cluster of independent restaurants, vintage shops, and music venues anchor the community. From there, residents can walk to Zilker Park, paddle on Lady Bird Lake, or hop on the MetroRapid bus to downtown in under fifteen minutes.

Schools include both Austin ISD-zoned campuses and a few sought-after charters. The mix of long-time residents, UT students, and tech transplants gives Austin Heights a community feel that's hard to manufacture.

What People Love
  • Bungalow architecture from the 1930s to 1950s
  • Walkable to Zilker Park, Barton Springs and Lady Bird Lake
  • Independent restaurants, breweries and music venues
  • Strong sense of community with annual block parties
