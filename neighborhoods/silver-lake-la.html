Silver Lake

Hillside cool with midcentury bones

A Neighborhood That Shaped LA

Silver Lake spreads across the hills east of Hollywood and west of Echo Park, ringing the 127-acre Silver Lake Reservoir. The neighborhood is famous for midcentury architecture (Neutra, Schindler, Lautner, Ain) tucked into impossibly steep streets, and for a creative class that made the area synonymous with LA cool decades before it was a hashtag.

Today daily life moves between Sunset Junction restaurants, the reservoir's 2.2-mile dog loop, and the independent shops along Hyperion. The Sunday farmers market remains one of the best in the city. Most homes are character single-family on tight lots; condos and duplex conversions sit closer to the flats.
